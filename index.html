Menstrual Cramps (Dysmenorrhea)

Having menstrual cramps is one of the most common, annoying parts of your period. They can strike right before or during that time of the month. Many women get them routinely. You’ll feel these cramps in your lower belly or back. They can range from mild to severe. They usually happen for the first time a year or two after a girl first gets her period.

With age, they usually become less painful and may stop entirely after you have your first baby. Your doctor may call your cramps “dysmenorrhea.”
The traditional treatment includes:

Pain relievers

Such as Ibuprofen оr anti-inflammatory drugs, such as Mefenamic Acid. The thing is, these pharmaceuticals can easily irritate your stomach and “contaminate” your body with its active ingredients.

Hormonal birth control

Which are basically pills, containing hormones that prevent ovulation and reduce the severity of menstrual cramps. This type of drugs can mess with your period and sometimes can make you chubby.

Surgery

Is the only option when yout menstrual cramps are caused by an underlying disorder. This condition is pretty rare, but in that case the surgical intervention can correct the problem and mat help reduce the symptoms.
